About Dunhamar

Dunhamar (pronounced /ˈdun.hə.ˌmɑr/) is a tool to help authors, gamemasters, and conlangers construct realistic fictional worlds in which their creations can thrive. It takes a random seed and several parameters and produces a stylized map, a historical timeline, and info sheets on each of the countries and languages. While no computer program can replace the joy and creativity of the entire worldbuilding process, Dunhamar can handle the parts that not all humans feel able or motivated to do.

Dunhamar is inspired by previous fantasy map generators like Azgaar's one, but has more of an emphasis on realism. It is not as interactive as some generators, and it does not generate as many fine details like mine locations and diplomatic relations. It does, however, account for several features missing in other generators that are noticeable on the macroscopic scale, such as planetary curvature, plate tectonics, and linguistic drift.
